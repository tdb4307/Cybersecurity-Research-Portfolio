Cybersecurity Research Portfolio
Timothy Bates | B.S. & M.S. Cybersecurity | University of West Florida
This repository collects academic research, technical reports, and applied projects completed throughout my undergraduate and graduate studies. The work spans offensive security, cloud architecture, corporate governance, and emerging AI threats.

Projects
1. Attack Surface Discovery in Penetration Testing
Course: CIS5396 – Ethical Hacking and Penetration Testing
A literature review and methodology paper examining the critical role of attack surface discovery within the penetration testing process. Uses IoT network architecture as a case study for applying zone-based analysis to enumerate vulnerabilities across layered ecosystems. Covers WHOIS lookups, DNS analysis, SSL certificate inspection, subdomain enumeration tools (Sublist3r, Amass, Assetfinder), network scanners (Nmap, Masscan), and web application scanners (OWASP ZAP, Burp Suite).
Key Topics: Reconnaissance methodology, attack surface mapping, black/gray/white box testing, IoT security, vulnerability enumeration

2. Penetration Testing Lab Report
Course: CIS5396 – Ethical Hacking and Penetration Testing
Hands-on penetration testing engagement conducted in a virtualized lab environment using Kali Linux and Metasploit. Performed subnet discovery with ifconfig, followed by progressive Nmap scans (-sV, -A, -O) against a /24 subnet to enumerate four live hosts. Identified vulnerabilities including open unencrypted protocols (FTP, HTTP), an exposed unauthorized MySQL instance, an outdated Windows 7/8 OS, and an open pfSense admin interface. Findings were documented and mapped to potential exploit vectors aligned with the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (GV.OC-01).
Key Topics: Network enumeration, service/version detection, OS fingerprinting, vulnerability identification, NIST CSF alignment

3. Cloud Computing Architecture and Security
Course: Cloud Computing Architecture and Security
Research paper exploring the design, implementation, and security implications of the three primary cloud service models — SaaS, PaaS, and IaaS — using Amazon Web Services as the primary reference. Frames security analysis around the CIA triad and addresses three core research questions: data confidentiality and integrity, regulatory compliance, and infrastructure availability under cyberattack. Examines specific AWS services including EC2, RDS, IAM, Cognito, KMS, CloudTrail, CloudWatch, Elastic Beanstalk, and Lambda.
Key Topics: Cloud service models, CIA triad, AWS security services, access control, encryption, compliance, DDoS resilience

4. Sony Interactive Entertainment Cybersecurity Case Study
Course: ISM 6575 – Legal and Ethical Aspects of Information Security
An in-depth analysis of cybersecurity management at Sony Interactive Entertainment, centered on the PlayStation Network infrastructure and two major security incidents. The 2011 PSN breach exposed PII for over 77 million users via SQL injection and social engineering. The 2023 MOVEit Transfer breach compromised employee SSNs through a supply chain attack by the Cl0p ransomware group. The paper examines both incidents through the lens of digital forensics, incident response, and corporate communication, then proposes solutions around data classification, PCI-DSS compliance, PR communication frameworks, and employee training.
Key Topics: Incident response, digital forensics, supply chain attacks, data classification, PCI-DSS, business continuity, corporate cybersecurity governance

5. The Rise of Generative AI and Its Impact on Cybersecurity
Course: Graduate Capstone
Comprehensive research paper examining the dual impact of Generative AI on the cybersecurity landscape. Covers the architecture of LLMs (GPTs), beneficial models (ChatGPT, Google Bard, DALL-E) and their defensive applications — threat detection, automated incident response, predictive analytics, and dynamic access control — alongside explicitly malicious models (WormGPT, FraudGPT) and their role in lowering the barrier for sophisticated cyberattacks. Addresses legal, ethical, and privacy concerns including regulatory gaps, GDPR compliance, intellectual property disputes, deepfakes, and accountability frameworks. Concludes with layered defensive strategies including AI-enhanced detection, zero-trust architecture, honeypots, red/purple teaming, and international regulatory collaboration.
Key Topics: Generative AI, LLMs, threat intelligence automation, WormGPT/FraudGPT, deepfakes, zero-trust, AI governance, GDPR, ethical AI frameworks
